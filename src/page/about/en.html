Uranus – a platform for a connected cultural landscape

Uranus is an open, collaboratively developed platform for event data. It understands culture not as a product, but as a living, diverse and socially relevant space. Its goal is to make cultural activities visible, connected and sustainably accessible – regardless of size, budget or reach.

Especially in a cultural landscape engaging with digital transformation, sustainability and participation, there is a need for tools that are not only efficient, but also value-driven. Uranus was created to fill this gap: as a digital infrastructure that enables collaboration, shares knowledge and strengthens cultural diversity.

Making culture visible – beyond market-driven logic

Many small, volunteer-run or non-commercial cultural offerings remain digitally invisible, even though they form the backbone of local cultural scenes. Uranus deliberately addresses this issue: all events are treated equally – regardless of budget, visibility or economic interest.

Through structured, open data, even small initiatives can present their activities on equal footing and distribute them across different channels – without additional costs and without dependency on commercial platforms.

Shaping digital transformation together

Uranus is open source and invites active participation. Municipalities, cultural institutions, independent initiatives and developers can jointly develop the platform and adapt it to their specific needs. Digitalisation is not consumed here – it is actively shaped.

  • Open interfaces for websites, apps, city portals or public displays
  • Reusable data instead of parallel, isolated solutions
  • Transparent structures instead of proprietary dependencies

Sustainable, accessible and participation-oriented

Sustainability also means using resources responsibly – both technically and organisationally. Uranus reduces duplicated data maintenance, facilitates cooperation and helps build long-term digital structures.

At the same time, the platform places strong emphasis on accessibility and inclusion: information about accessibility, target groups or conditions helps people find suitable cultural offerings and participate in them.

For a diverse and connected cultural future

Uranus does not see itself as a finished product, but as a shared project. An invitation to everyone who envisions culture as open, solidaristic and future-oriented – and who wants to use digital tools to expand cultural participation, rather than limit it.

Who Uranus is for

Uranus is aimed at people and organisations who understand culture as a public, collective and shapeable space. The platform brings together very different actors without reducing them to roles such as “providers” and “consumers”.

Cultural initiatives & independent scenes

For independent groups, initiatives, collectives, associations and individual practitioners, Uranus offers a way to make events visible without having to submit to commercial platform logics. Small, experimental or volunteer-driven activities gain a digital presence that creates reach without adding administrative burden.

Cultural institutions & venues

Museums, theatres, socio-cultural centres, libraries, clubs and other cultural venues can use Uranus to publish their events in a structured and sustainable way. Instead of maintaining data multiple times, content can be managed centrally and reused in different contexts – from an organisation’s own website to municipal or regional portals.

Municipalities, networks & cultural administration

For cities, communities and cultural policy actors, Uranus provides a transparent foundation to make cultural activity visible, analyse it and develop it strategically. The platform supports cultural policies focused on participation, diversity and long-term structures, rather than short-term individual projects.

Audiences & culturally interested people

People who want to experience culture find a low-threshold entry point into a diverse cultural landscape through Uranus. Filters by interests, location, time or accessibility make orientation easier and foster participation – including beyond well-established and heavily promoted offerings.

Developers & digital practitioners

As an open-source project, Uranus also addresses people who want to help shape digital tools for culture. Open interfaces, transparent data models and a collaborative development approach invite the creation of new applications, visualisations and innovative use cases.

A platform for collaboration

At its core, Uranus is for everyone who does not think of culture in isolation, but as a connected, solidaristic and learning system. The platform aims to build bridges – between institutions and independent scenes, between analogue practice and digital infrastructure, and between local initiatives and broader visibility.
